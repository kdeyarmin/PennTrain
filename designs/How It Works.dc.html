How it works

Set it up once. It nags so you don't have to.

Every module — training, residents, incidents, maintenance — follows the same four moves, so staff learn it once.

1
Configure the operation

Add facilities, roles, residents, and employees. Import your roster by CSV. Set the training, credential, and alert rules that apply.

A PCH direct care worker automatically gets the 12-hour §2600.65 bucket.
2
Route the work

Training, resident services, schedules, incident follow-up, and approvals go to the person responsible — each with an owner and a deadline.

A hospital return opens notification, reassessment, and plan-review tasks.
3
Capture proof as it happens

Completions, signatures, uploads, sign-ins, and audit events attach to the right employee, resident, requirement, and date.

A QR class check-in becomes logged in-service hours instantly.
4
See risk, share evidence

Dashboards and escalating alerts surface gaps early. Binders and evidence rooms answer leadership, auditors, and surveyors.

The binder PDF rebuilds from live records in one click.

Setup is self-serve — most single facilities are entering real records the same day.

Switching without the drama

✓An afternoon, not a project. CSV import brings your whole roster in; your binder stays untouched while you ramp.
✓Staff need only a browser. QR check-in from their own phones, a phone-installable training player — no app store, no IT.
✓Run both for month one. Keep the paper binder alongside until CareBase has earned your trust.
✓Your data leaves with you. Export everything if you cancel — records are yours, not hostages.
A week with CareBase

What actually changes, day by day

MONDAY

A new aide is hired. Her 12-hour §2600.65 plan, orientation checklist, Act 34 countdown, and TB screen are assigned before lunch — nobody built a spreadsheet row.

TUESDAY

Mr. Alvarez returns from the hospital. Provider notification, reassessment, and support-plan review open automatically, each with an owner and a clock.

WEDNESDAY

Dementia in-service, 2 p.m. Staff scan the rotating QR at the door; the hours land on each record the moment they sign in. No paper sheet to file.

THURSDAY

Second-shift fire drill, east wing. Logged from a phone during the drill — evacuation time, every §2600.132 field, PDF filed before the shift ends.

FRIDAY

Corporate asks how the facility looks. You export the binder PDF from live records and go home on time. That's the product.
