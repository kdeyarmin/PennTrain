# Product Naming Research

Research into a new product name for the platform currently branded **CareMetric Train**
(internal codename "PennTrain").

## Brief

CareMetric's product family uses the pattern **CareMetric + one evocative word**:
**Breathe** (CPAP software) and **Practice** (physician EMR). The new name should follow
that convention.

The brief evolved across the research:

1. The product began as an online learning platform but is now an all-in-one platform for
   personal care homes and Assisted Living Facilities (ALFs) — EMR, tracking, online
   learning, compliance, and more.
2. Purely evocative names were set aside in favor of names that **identify what the
   software does**.
3. Words that mean **home or facility** are of particular interest.

Product facts grounding the research (see `README.md`,
`artifacts/caremetric-train/src/components/marketing/content.ts`, `src/pages/Landing.tsx`,
`src/lib/facilityTypes.ts`): training matrix and courses, competencies,
credentials/clearances, resident care compliance (RASP/ASP), incidents, fire drills,
scheduling/workforce, and a one-click survey-ready compliance binder. The marketing copy
already describes the product as a "Compliance Command Center" with the hero line
"Know who is trained, what is due, and where the proof lives."

## Names to avoid (conflict research)

Software and services already using nearby names:

- **Competing platforms:** Relias, MedTrainer, CareAcademy, HealthStream, MatrixCare,
  GoAudits, MyFieldAudits, Eldermark, ECP, Sentrics, Caremerge, Home Care Pulse,
  Cornerstone Learning → avoid *Academy, Trainer, Pulse, Matrix, Audit, Stream,
  Sentinel/Sentry, Cornerstone*.
- **Senior-care organizations:** Sunrise Senior Living, Kindred Healthcare, Honor (home
  care), Thrive Senior Living, Comfort Keepers, The Arbor Company.
- **"CareBase" specifically** (evaluated at the user's request): taken several times over —
  CareBase Health (all-in-one home care agency platform), myCareBase (caregiver matching),
  Carebase Solutions (clinic software), Carebase UK (residential care operator since 1999).
  Standalone "CareBase" is not viable; the compound **CareMetric Base** remains viable and
  distinctive.
- **Other collisions flagged inline below:** Homebase (scheduling app), Microsoft Copilot,
  Google Nest, Adobe/Abode, Sage (accounting), Estia Health, Dannon Oikos, Porch.com.

## Leading recommendation

**CareMetric Base** — the strongest synthesis of the brief:

- **Means home:** "home base," for software serving personal care *homes*. Tagline writes
  itself: *"The home base for personal care homes and ALFs."*
- **Identifies function:** the base everything runs on — records, training, compliance,
  scheduling; carries the foundation/system-of-record meaning without jargon.
- **Fits the family:** Breathe, Practice, Base — short, humble, one-word.
- **Flags:** frame as home-base/HQ (never a pricing tier named "Base," which reads as the
  entry-level trim); conversational shorthand "CareBase" brushes against CareBase Health.

Runners-up: **Command** (most operational; already the product's own language),
**One** (plainest all-in-one signal), **Campus** (facility + place of learning),
**Rounds** (warm, industry-native oversight), **Hale** (Hawaiian *home* + "hale and
hearty"), **Domus** (Latin *home*).

A strategic option worth considering: an umbrella + modules architecture — e.g.
**CareMetric Base** (or One/Command) as the platform, with **Train**, **Chart**, and
**Comply** as named modules inside it. This preserves existing Train brand equity while
making the all-in-one scope explicit.

## Round 1 — compliance & training verbs (original brief)

All names read as "CareMetric ___".

### Tier 1 — strongest fits
1. **Ready** — survey-readiness is the hero promise; calm, confident sibling to Breathe.
2. **Prove** — "where the proof lives"; owns the evidence-first differentiator.
3. **Assure** — compliance assurance + emotional reassurance for operators.
4. **Attest** — precise regulatory verb; policy attestation is literally a feature.
5. **Steady** — "steady hands"; calm operator confidence.
6. **Prepare** — what the platform does every day before survey day.
7. **Qualify** — the Administrator Qualification module + qualified staff.
8. **Clear** — clearances (Act 34/73/33), "all clear," clarity of dashboards.
9. **Uphold** — upholding standards of care.
10. **Comply** — bluntest category signal; unambiguous but cold.

### Tier 2 — training & competence
11. **Equip** 12. **Orient** 13. **Certify** 14. **Verify** 15. **Muster** ("pass muster" +
roll-call echo of QR attendance) 16. **Coach** 17. **Mentor** 18. **Renew** 19. **Tend**
20. **Abide**

### Tier 3 — evocative nouns
21. **Compass** 22. **Keystone** (PA + holds the arch together; crowded among PA orgs)
23. **Harbor** ("safe harbor") 24. **Beacon** 25. **Anchor** 26. **Atlas** 27. **Keeper**
28. **Vault** 29. **Passport** 30. **Ledger** 31. **Merit** 32. **Cadence** 33. **Hearth**

10. **Hestia** (Greek goddess of hearth and home; flag: Estia Health) 11. **Homeroom**
12. **Heim** 13. **Lodge** 14. **Hogar** 15. **Abode** (flag: Adobe) 16. **Haus**
